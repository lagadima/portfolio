Stopping people from giving up payment

My role

Research: I investigated the problem space with the market researcher and the data analyst to understand opportunities.

Build and test: I quickly designed and involved stakeholders throughout the process. I tested the final design with real users.

Impact: I presented the design to the leadership team, planned the soft launch of the first steps which will impact 13k current players and 95k potential players.

TEAM: 1 data analyst, 1 market researcher, 1 PM, 1 marketing specialist, 1 UX researcher

TIMELINE: JUN 2020 - AUG 2020

TOOL: Miro, Figma, Zoom

Takeaways

Move forward with uncertainty, the nature of product design is iteration: in checkout domain, there are so many grey areas and risks that make people too nervous to proceed. Before wrapping up market research, stakeholder interviews and competitive analysis, there were some time I paused designing but focusing on research, but soon I realized that we can never be confident enough about anything. I changed my approach and started to create concepts, diagrams, and prototypes to show PMs what we learn, have developers evaluate dev efforts. "Show instead of tell" is the value designers should bring to the team.

Navigate through complicated organizations: working in such a huge organization like EA taught me a mindset that: don't block people, and consider the possibility that people might block you.

Incorporate the design into the whole service picture: In this design solution, I only focused on the design of the digital product, but considered less about the whole picture: how to initiate the feeding of initial users and meetups? How to encourage mentors to commit more time to helping others?

Take initiative and make an impact:: **Take initiative and make an impact**: unlike in school settings where the design process is strictly followed and in my previous internships where PMs have a lot of say.

At EA Product Designers are expected to think like PMs and act like PMs. I took the initiative to communicate with team, served as a point of contact for design relevant resources, planned the work when I identify a key missing part with my team, and set goals with my PM and teammates.
